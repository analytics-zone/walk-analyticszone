How to Track Your Walking

Method 1: Cadence-Based (Recommended)

Why Cadence?

Cadence (steps/min) is 23-35% more accurate than speed-based estimates for intensity, and it's independent of stride length variability or terrain.

How to Use

  • No tech needed: Count steps for 60 seconds while walking normally
  • With smartwatch/app: WalkAnalytics auto-detects cadence from accelerometer
  • Targets:
    • Light: 60-90 spm
    • Moderate: 100-110 spm (โ3 METs)
    • Vigorous: 120-130+ spm (โ6 METs)

Method 2: Daily Step Count

  • Smartphone pedometer: iPhone Health, Google Fit (built-in, free)
  • Wearable: Apple Watch, Fitbit, Garmin
  • Target: 7,000-8,000 steps/day for health
  • Limitation: Doesn't distinguish intensity โ 7,000 slow steps โ 7,000 brisk steps for health impact

Method 3: Time + Intensity

  • Track minutes: Log "moderate" vs "vigorous" time manually
  • Target: 150 min moderate OR 75 min vigorous per week
  • Best for: People without step-tracking devices

Method 4: Heart Rate-Based

  • Moderate: 64-76% of max HR (calculate: 220 - age)
  • Vigorous: 77-93% of max HR
  • Tool: Chest strap or wrist-based HR monitor
  • Limitation: HR affected by heat, hydration, stress, medications

โ Our Recommendation: Cadence + Steps

Track both total daily steps AND cadence during intentional walking sessions. This gives you:

  • Volume: Total steps/day (overall activity level)
  • Intensity: Cadence during walks (health benefit proxy)
  • Actionable: Easy to adjust ("walk faster" = increase cadence)
