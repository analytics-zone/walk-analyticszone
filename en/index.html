What makes WalkAnalytics different from other walking apps?

What is Peak-30 cadence and why does it matter?

Peak-30 cadence is your average steps per minute during the best 30 consecutive minutes of walking each day. A breakthrough study of 78,500 people (Del Pozo-Cruz, JAMA 2022) showed it independently predicts mortality risk‚Äîeven after controlling for total daily steps. We track this automatically, something no other app does.

How does ACWR prevent injuries?

Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio compares your recent training (last 7 days) to your longer-term average (28 days). Research shows ACWR >1.50 increases injury risk 2-4 times. We calculate this daily and warn you before dangerous spikes, helping you progress safely with 5-10% weekly increases.

Why cadence zones instead of heart rate zones?

Cadence is more reliable and practical. The CADENCE-Adults study proved 100 spm = moderate intensity (3 METs) with 86% sensitivity and 90% specificity. Heart rate varies with heat, stress, illness, caffeine‚Äîcadence doesn't. Plus, you don't need a chest strap or watch; just count steps.

Can WalkAnalytics help with fall prevention?

Yes. We track clinical fall risk indicators: gait speed <0.8 m/s, double support >35%, asymmetry (GSI) >10%, and walking steadiness. These are evidence-based thresholds from geriatric research. Early detection allows intervention before falls occur‚Äîcritical for adults 65+.

How does WalkAnalytics protect my privacy?

All data stays on your iPhone‚Äîperiod. No cloud sync, no accounts, no servers receiving your health data. We process everything locally using on-device algorithms. No. WalkAnalytics works with iPhone or Apple Watch. For basic metrics (cadence, distance, steps), just your phone. For heart rate data and more accurate metrics, an Apple Watch helps but isn't required. We integrate with Apple Health, so any compatible device works.

Absolutely. We're used by rehab patients tracking recovery objectively. The Gait Symmetry Index (GSI) formula quantifies left-right differences (<3% normal, >10% clinically significant).
